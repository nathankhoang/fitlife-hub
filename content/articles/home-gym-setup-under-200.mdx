You don't need a $10,000 home gym to get in great shape. With under $200 and these five pieces of equipment, you can do virtually any workout — strength, cardio, mobility, and more.

## The $200 Home Gym List

| Equipment | Price |
|---|---|
| Resistance Bands (5-pack) | ~$12 |
| Pull-Up Bar | ~$30 |
| Yoga / Workout Mat | ~$25 |
| Foam Roller | ~$35 |
| Adjustable Dumbbells (optional upgrade) | ~$100+ |

Total for basics: **~$100**
With adjustable dumbbells: **~$200**

## #1 — Resistance Bands (~$12)

Resistance bands are arguably the most versatile piece of home gym equipment you can own. They add resistance to squats, push-ups, rows, and dozens of other movements.

**What you can do with them:**
- Banded squats and glute bridges
- Resistance push-ups
- Rows and pull-aparts
- Lateral band walks
- Bicep curls and tricep extensions

## #2 — Pull-Up Bar (~$30)

A doorframe pull-up bar unlocks an entirely different category of upper-body training. Pull-ups, chin-ups, hanging knee raises — it's a vertical pulling machine that fits in any doorframe.

<AffiliateProductCard productId="pull-up-bar" />

**What you can do with it:**
- Pull-ups and chin-ups
- Hanging ab exercises
- Rows (with a towel or sheet)
- Shoulder decompression stretches

## #3 — Workout Mat (~$25–$120)

A quality mat makes floor work dramatically more comfortable. It also protects hardwood floors during dumbbell work.

<AffiliateProductCard productId="yoga-mat" />

**What you can do with it:**
- Core work (planks, crunches, leg raises)
- Yoga and mobility routines
- Push-up and dip surfaces
- Stretching and cool-down work

## #4 — Foam Roller (~$35)

Recovery is just as important as training. A foam roller helps reduce muscle soreness, improve flexibility, and speed up recovery between sessions.

<AffiliateProductCard productId="foam-roller" />

**How to use it:**
- Roll each major muscle group for 30–60 seconds before and after workouts
- Focus on quads, hamstrings, calves, upper back, and IT bands
- Stop and hold on tender spots for 10–20 seconds

## #5 — Adjustable Dumbbells (Optional Upgrade)

If you have the budget, adjustable dumbbells are the most powerful upgrade for a home gym. One set replaces an entire dumbbell rack.

## Sample Home Gym Workout

**Circuit (3 rounds, 45 seconds on / 15 seconds rest):**
1. Banded squat jumps
2. Push-ups
3. Pull-ups (or band rows)
4. Glute bridges with band
5. Plank hold
6. Mountain climbers

## Where to Buy Used Equipment

Before buying new, check:
- **Facebook Marketplace** — people buy gym equipment and never use it
- **Craigslist** — often 50–70% off retail
- **OfferUp** — great for local deals on dumbbells and barbells

## Conclusion

A $100–$200 home gym is more than enough to make real progress. The key is consistency, not fancy equipment. Start with the basics — bands, a pull-up bar, and a mat — and build from there as your budget and space allow.
