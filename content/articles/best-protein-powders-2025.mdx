## What to Look For in a Protein Powder

Before buying, consider:

- **Protein per serving** — aim for 20–30g
- **Leucine content** — drives muscle protein synthesis
- **Ingredients** — avoid excessive fillers and artificial sweeteners
- **Taste and mixability** — you have to actually drink it
- **Price per serving** — calculate cost per gram of protein

## Types of Protein Powder Explained

### Whey Concentrate
The most common and affordable form. Contains some lactose and fat. Great for post-workout.

### Whey Isolate
Further filtered to remove most lactose and fat. Higher protein percentage per serving. Better for lactose-sensitive individuals.

### Casein
Digests slowly — ideal for a nighttime protein source to fuel overnight muscle repair.

### Plant-Based
Pea, rice, hemp blends for those avoiding dairy. Look for blended plant proteins for a complete amino acid profile.

## How Much Protein Do You Actually Need?

Research suggests **0.7–1g of protein per pound of bodyweight** is optimal for muscle building. For a 160-pound person, that's 112–160g of protein per day.

Protein powder should supplement your diet, not replace whole food sources. Prioritize chicken, eggs, fish, beef, and legumes.

## When to Take Protein Powder

- **Post-workout** — within 1–2 hours after training
- **Morning** — if your breakfast is low in protein
- **Between meals** — to hit daily targets

## Common Mistakes

1. **Relying only on powder** — real food protein sources are more satiating
2. **Taking too much** — more than 40g in one sitting provides diminishing returns
3. **Ignoring total daily intake** — the timing matters less than the total
