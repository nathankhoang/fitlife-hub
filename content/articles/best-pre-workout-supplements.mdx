Pre-workout supplements can meaningfully improve your training sessions — but only if they're well-formulated. Most products on the market are underdosed, overhyped, or loaded with ineffective ingredients. Here's what actually works.

## What Makes a Good Pre-Workout?

A quality pre-workout should contain:

| Ingredient | Evidence-Based Dose | Effect |
|---|---|---|
| Caffeine | 150–300mg | Energy, focus, performance |
| Citrulline Malate | 6–8g | Pump, reduced fatigue |
| Beta-Alanine | 3.2g | Muscular endurance |
| Creatine Monohydrate | 3–5g | Strength, power output |
| L-Theanine | 100–200mg | Smooth energy (reduces jitters) |

## Pre-Workout Ingredients to Avoid

- **Proprietary blends** — you can't verify if ingredients are properly dosed
- **DMAA/DMHA** — stimulants with serious cardiovascular risks
- **Excessive caffeine (>400mg)** — diminishing returns and anxiety
- **Artificial dyes** — no performance benefit, potential health concerns

## How to Use Pre-Workout Effectively

1. **Start with half a serving** to assess your caffeine tolerance
2. **Take 20–30 minutes before training** — allows ingredients to kick in
3. **Cycle off every 6–8 weeks** to prevent caffeine tolerance buildup
4. **Avoid taking after 3pm** if you train in the evening — disrupts sleep
5. **Don't rely on it every session** — train some days without it

## Pre-Workout Alternatives

If you prefer to avoid heavy stimulant products:

- **Black coffee** — 100–200mg caffeine, cheap, proven
- **Creatine alone** — no stimulants, consistent performance gains
- **Citrulline + caffeine stack** — build your own minimal formula

## The Stimulant-Free Option

If you work out in the evening or are caffeine-sensitive, look for stim-free pre-workouts containing only citrulline, beta-alanine, and betaine. They provide the pump and endurance benefits without disrupting sleep.
